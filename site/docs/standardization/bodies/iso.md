# ISO/IEC

The International Organization for Standardization (ISO) and the International Electrotechnical Commission (IEC) jointly develop international standards through JTC 1. This document outlines the pathway for ADL standardization through ISO/IEC.

:::note Timeline Consideration
ISO/IEC standardization typically takes 3-5 years. Consider a dual-track approach with faster open-source standardization (LF/IETF) alongside ISO work.
:::

## Overview

| Attribute | Details |
|-----------|---------|
| Organization | ISO/IEC JTC 1 |
| Relevant Subcommittee | SC 42 (Artificial Intelligence) |

| Timeline | 3-5+ years |
| Cost | National body membership fees |

## Why ISO/IEC?

- **Global recognition:** International standards with broad adoption
- **Regulatory alignment:** Often referenced in regulations
- **Cross-industry:** Applicable across sectors
- **AI focus:** SC 42 specifically covers AI standards

## Relevant Subcommittees

### JTC 1/SC 42 - Artificial Intelligence

Primary candidate for ADL:

- AI terminology and concepts (ISO/IEC 22989)
- AI management systems (ISO/IEC 42001)
- AI risk management (ISO/IEC 23894)

### JTC 1/SC 38 - Cloud Computing

Potential for cloud-deployed agents:

- Cloud service agreements
- Interoperability standards

## ISO/IEC Process

1. **New Work Item Proposal (NWIP):** Formal proposal through national body
2. **Working Draft (WD):** Initial specification development
3. **Committee Draft (CD):** Working group review
4. **Draft International Standard (DIS):** Broader ISO review
5. **Final Draft International Standard (FDIS):** Final ballot
6. **International Standard (IS):** Published standard

## Challenges

- **Timeline:** 3-5 years is common
- **Access:** Participation requires national body involvement
- **Cost:** Standards are not freely available
- **Complexity:** Formal process with specific requirements

## Alignment Strategy

Consider dual-track approach:

1. Open specification through LF/IETF (freely available)
2. ISO/IEC standardization for regulatory contexts
